Over the 4 years, the class average fluctuated slightly. We notice that IG2 and A Level averages were higher than the year prior to them. One likely cause is the transition to online classes in those years. Similar to all education systems around the world, SIS was affected by grade inflation.

This can further be seen in the graph above. The proportion of students who had and A or above was significantly higher in IG2 and A Levels, the two years that was affected by Covid-19. It is also interesting to see that IG1 was statistically the most difficult year. This may be because of the change to the Cambridge International curriculum. Students had been studying the Singaporean curriculum prior to IG1.

Failures

31% of respondents said they failed a subject. 3 students preferred not to disclose. Failures, however, were a minor setback. At least 92% of students had a passing predicted grade for their A-Level final exams.
